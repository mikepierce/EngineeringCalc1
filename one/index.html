Overview of Limits & Continuity

In the beginning of the story of calculus there are some minor characters we must meet. These characters’ primary importance is to introduce us to the protagonists of calculus, differentiation and the integration, but we should know them in their own right first. The first of these minor characters is the limit.

  • Concept

    Understand what the notation \( \lim\limits_{x \to x_0} f(x) \) means.

  • Skill

    Be able to compute \( \lim\limits_{x \to x_0} f(x) \) either numerically or precisely.

The important idea behind introducing the limit is that we need language and notation for talking what what’s happening around a point \(x_0\), instead of directly at the point. While discussing the purpose of the course, we’ll gradually reveal the limit’s relationship to the derivative and how it can help us find the slope of a line tangent to a curve at a given point.

The other of minor character is the concept of continuity of a function.

  • Concept

    Understand what it means for a function to be continuous on some domain, and why many natural phenomenon morally should be modelled a continuous function.

We can only apply the techniques of calculus, define the derivative and integral of a function on some domain as a formula, if we take the hypothesis that the function is continuous on that domain.
