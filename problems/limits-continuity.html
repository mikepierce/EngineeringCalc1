Limits & Continuity

  1. Evaluate each limit, or determine that the limit doesn’t exist. \[ \lim\limits_{x \to 4} \frac{2x-9}{x+4} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to -4} \frac{3x^2+9x-5}{x^2-16} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 100} \frac{x-100}{10-\sqrt{x}} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{x^2+1}\right) \] \[ \lim\limits_{x \to -3} \frac{|x+3|}{2x+6} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 0.25^{-}} \frac{4x-1}{|4x^3-x^2|} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 0^-} \left(\frac{1}{|x|} - \frac{1}{x}\right) \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 0^+} \left(\frac{1}{|x|} - \frac{1}{x}\right) \]
  2. Using the fact that \(\lim_{x \to 0} \sin(x)/x = 1\) and the “limit laws" demonstrate how to algebraically find the values of the following limits. \[ \lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(3x)}{7x} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(3x)}{\sin(7x)} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{x\cos(x)}{\tan(x)} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin^2(2x)}{x^2} \]
  3. Without referencing technology, determine the value of each limit. \[ \lim\limits_{x \to \infty} \sqrt{x} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 7^+} \frac{x^2-x-40}{x-7} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 7^-} \frac{x^2-3x-28}{x-7} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 7} \frac{49}{(x-7)^2} \] \[ \lim\limits_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2-7x+5}{2x-1} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2-7x+5}{(2x-1)^2} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2-7x+5}{(2x-1)^3} \]
  4. Demonstrate how to evaluate each of these limits. Hint: “conjugate” \[ \lim\limits_{x \to 1} \frac{1-\sqrt{x}}{1-x} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{1-\sqrt{1-x^2}}{x} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{1-\sqrt{1-x^2}}{x^2} \]
  5. Evaluate each limit, or determine that the limit doesn’t exist. \[ \lim\limits_{x \to -\infty} f(x) \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to -2} f(x) \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to -1^-} f(x) \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to -1^+} f(x) \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to -1} f(x) \] \[ \lim\limits_{x \to 0} f(x) \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 1} f(x) \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 2} f(x) \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to \infty} f(x) \]
  6. Let \(g\) be the function defined piecewise as \[ g(x) = \begin{cases} -x &\text{for } x \lt 1 \qquad &7 &\text{for } x=1 \\x^2-2 &\text{for } 1 \lt x \leq 5 \qquad &7x-5 &\text{for } 5 \lt x \end{cases} \]

    Evaluate the following. If a limit doesn’t exist, say so.

    \[ \displaystyle \lim\limits_{x \to 1^-} g(x) \quad \displaystyle \lim\limits_{x \to 1} g(x) \quad \displaystyle g(1) \quad \displaystyle \lim\limits_{x \to 5^-} g(x) \quad \displaystyle \lim\limits_{x \to 5^+} g(x) \quad \displaystyle \lim\limits_{x \to 5} g(x) \]
  7. Use a computing device to approximate the value of these limits accurate to seven decimal places. \[ \lim\limits_{x \to \infty} x^{\frac{1}{x}} \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to 0} \; 4\arctan\!\left(x^x\right) \qquad \lim\limits_{x \to \infty} \left(\frac{x}{x+1}\right)^x \]
  8. For functions \(f\) and \(g,\) it may be that \(\lim_{x \to c}\big(f(x) + g(x)\big)\) exists even though neither \(\lim_{x \to c}f(x)\) nor \(\lim_{x \to c}g(x)\) exist. Find examples of such functions.
  9. For functions \(f\) and \(g,\) it may be that \(\lim_{x \to c}\big(f(x)g(x)\big)\) exists even though neither \(\lim_{x \to c}f(x)\) nor \(\lim_{x \to c}g(x)\) exist. Find examples of such functions.
  10. Let \(g\) be the function defined piecewise as \[ g(x) = \begin{cases} -1 &\text{ for } x \lt -3 \\f(x) &\text{ for } -3 \leq x \lt 1 \\-x^2+5 &\text{ for } x \geq 1 \end{cases} \]
    1. Find an example of a linear function \(f\) such that \(g\) is continuous.
    2. Find an example of a non-linear function \(f\) such that \(g\) is continuous.
  11. How can you very quickly prove that the polynomial \(x^{171}-7x^2-1\) has a root on the domain \([0,2]\) by invoking the Intermediate Value Theorem?

Challenges

  1. James Stewart

    Does there exist a number that is exactly one more than its cube?
  2. If \(\lim_{x \to c} \big(f(x) + g(x)\big) = 11\) and \(\lim_{x \to c} \big(f(x) - g(x)\big) = 7,\) what must the value of \(\lim_{x \to c} \big(f(x)g(x)\big)\) be?

  3. True or False?

    Suppose that a function \(f\) defined on some domain \([a,b]\) has the following property: for any \(N\) between \(f(a)\) and \(f(b)\) there exists some \(c\) between \(a\) and \(b\) such that \(f(c) = N.\) Then \(f\) must be a continuous function.
